## 2. Some important notes for understanding the Brownian Motion: (any mathematical symbols will be represented in the LaTeX language for a better reading)
- Brownian Motion can be seen as paths, who strongly oscillate. That means, no matter where you look at the graph, there will not be any leaps, but always continuous, highly irregular fluctuations at every scale, so that the path never becomes smooth or differentiable.
- The starting point of these graphs are always at 0.
- Mathematically you have a sequence of normally distributed random variables, whose independent increments have mean zero and variance proportional to the time step.
- Brownian Motion are the basics of stochastic analysis and finance mathematics for modelling stock prices.
